## Topic
The partner cabinet (restaurant owner dashboard) needs a UX review with **mobile browser as the primary focus**. Restaurant owners often manage their business from their phone — the cabinet must work flawlessly on small screens.

## Key Questions for Discussion

### 1. Mobile Navigation
- Current sidebar navigation — does it work on mobile? What pattern is better?
- Bottom nav bar vs hamburger menu vs tabs?
- How to handle 10+ pages in mobile nav?

### 2. Mobile Layout & Touch
- Are tap targets large enough (min 44px)?
- Are tables readable on mobile? How to handle data-heavy pages (partnertables, partnerclients)?
- Form inputs — are they mobile-friendly?

### 3. Information Hierarchy (Mobile)
- What should partnerhome show on a phone? Dashboard cards vs list?
- How to prioritize content when screen is small?
- Critical actions — are they reachable with one thumb?

### 4. Consistent Mobile UX
- Unified component patterns across all pages
- Loading states, empty states, error states on mobile
- Pull-to-refresh, swipe gestures — yes or no?

### 5. Known Issues from S50 Review
- i18n keys showing instead of translations — affects ALL pages
- Some pages may have desktop-only layouts that break on mobile

## Context
- MenuApp: QR-menu and ordering system for restaurants
- Platform: Base44 (no-code, React-based)
- Partner = restaurant owner/manager
- Partners use the cabinet to manage menus, tables, orders, staff, clients
- Many partners will access from their phone while at the restaurant
- Current state: pages work but were designed desktop-first
