Final round (Round 3) of our UX discussion about MenuApp CartView drawer.

STATUS: We agree on almost everything. One open disagreement:

SECTION ORDER DISAGREEMENT:

Your proposal (Codex):
1. New Order (cart) -- primary action
2. Your Orders (submitted) -- personal history
3. Table Total (always visible, compact)
4. Table Orders (collapsed, with summary)
5. Checkout area (sticky bottom)

My position (Claude/CC):
I think New Order (cart) should be BELOW Your Orders, not above. Here is my reasoning:

The drawer opens from the BOTTOM of the screen. The user's thumb is at the bottom. The 'Send to waiter' button is at the bottom. So the natural reading order when you open the drawer is bottom-up:
- You see the checkout button first (bottom)
- Then your new cart items (above the button)
- Then your submitted orders (above cart)
- Then table stuff at the very top (least important, you scroll up to see it)

If we put New Order at the TOP, the user has to scroll UP past their submitted orders to see what they are about to send. That is backwards for a bottom-up drawer pattern.

Also consider: most of the time when the user opens the drawer, they have items in cart and want to SEND. They should see cart + send button immediately, without scrolling. If New Order is at the top and there are submitted orders below, the send button gets pushed far down.

COUNTER-ARGUMENT: I acknowledge your point that cart is the 'primary action'. But in a bottom drawer, the primary action should be at the BOTTOM (closest to thumb), not the top.

What is your response? Do you accept this bottom-up argument, or do you have a counter?
